The Complete Driving License (DL) Application Journey and Prerequisite Roadmap: Obtaining a driving license in Dehradun is a two-phase journey. You cannot apply for a permanent Driving License (DL) directly. Phase 1 is the Learner's License (LL). The applicant must apply for and pass the Learner's License test first. Once the LL is issued, the applicant must hold it for a mandatory waiting period of 30 days (and within 180 days) before they become eligible to initiate Phase 2, which is applying for the Permanent Driving License (DL) and taking the physical driving test on the track. Any general inquiry about getting a license should outline both phases sequentially.

Learner's License (LL) Minimum Age and Eligibility Slabs: Minimum age requirements for getting a Learner's License in Dehradun are strictly enforced. For driving motor vehicles without gear up to 50cc, the minimum age is 16 years, with written consent of parents or guardians. For private cars (LMV) and light motor vehicles, as well as motorcycles with gear, the minimum age is 18 years. For transport and heavy commercial vehicles (trucks, buses), the applicant must be at least 20 years old and must hold a valid LMV license for at least 1 year.

Learner's License (LL) Online Application Process: Go to the official Sarathi Parivahan website (sarathi.parivahan.gov.in) and choose "Uttarakhand" as the state. Click on "Apply for Learner Licence". Fill out the personal details, address details, and select vehicle classes. Upload digital scanned copies of Age Proof (Passport, Birth Certificate, or High School Marksheet) and Address Proof (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, or Utility Bills). Pay the fee of Rs. 200 (or Rs. 400 for smart card license) using online net banking, debit/credit cards, or UPI. Once paid, book an appointment slot for the online screen test (LL Test). On the selected slot, take the online test from home (if Aadhaar-based e-KYC is used) or visit the RTO Dehradun office test lab. The screen test contains 15 questions about road signs and safety rules; you must score at least 12 correct answers to pass and download the LL certificate online instantly.

Learner's License (LL) Offline Application Process: Visit the Regional Transport Office (RTO) Dehradun at Rajpur Road. Purchase Form 1 (physical fitness self-declaration) and Form 2 (application form) from the counter. Fill out the forms in block letters. Attach self-attested photocopies of Age/ID Proof (10th Class Marksheet to verify Name, Father's Name spelling, and Date of Birth) and Address Proof (Aadhaar or Voter ID). Submit the physical file at the document verification counter (Counter 1). The RTO officer will check the documents and approve the file. Go to the cash counter and pay the LL fee in cash. Collect the fee receipt containing your application number. Take your receipt and file for initial document checking at Room 26 or 27, get your photo and signature captured (biometrics) at the desk inside Room 25, and then stand in the queue to take the computer-based screen test inside the actual LL testing lab located in Room 29. If you pass, the RTO office will print the physical LL paper copy and hand it to you, or you can print it later from the website.

Permanent Driving License (DL) Online Application Process: You are eligible to apply for a permanent DL online only after holding a valid Learner's License for at least 30 days and within 180 days from the LL issue date. Go to the Sarathi Parivahan portal (sarathi.parivahan.gov.in) and select "Apply for Driving Licence". Enter your Learner's License number and Date of Birth. Fill out the application Form 4 details. Upload digital scans of your original Learner's License, age proof, and address proof. Pay the license fee of Rs. 500 (or Rs. 700 for smart card DL) online. Once the payment is verified, book a slot for the physical Driving Skill Test at the RTO Dehradun test track. Print the slot booking receipt. On the test day, go to the RTO Dehradun driving track with your registered vehicle, original documents, fee receipt, and slot confirmation. If you pass the physical driving test under the inspector's supervision, your smart card DL is printed and dispatched to your home address via Speed Post within 7 to 14 days.

Permanent Driving License (DL) Offline Application Process: Visit the RTO Dehradun office. Purchase Form 4 (application form for DL) and Form 1A (medical certificate, mandatory if applying for a transport/commercial vehicle or if the applicant is over 40 years old). Form 1A must be signed and stamped by a registered government medical practitioner. Assemble the physical file containing: Form 4, signed Form 1A, original Learner's License (held for at least 30 days), self-attested copies of Aadhaar, age proof, and 3 passport-size photos. Submit the file and complete your biometrics (photo & signature) in Room 25 or Room 26 (with Room 25 serving as the central hub for biometrics). Once biometrics are registered, get the final file approval from the licensing authority in Room 27. Pay the license fee (Rs. 500, or Rs. 700 for smart card) in cash at the cash counter. The clerk will issue a manual fee receipt and allocate a driving test date. On the scheduled test day, report to the Dehradun RTO driving track with your vehicle, original documents (RC, insurance, and PUC of the vehicle you are testing with), and the fee receipt. Show the receipt to the motor vehicle inspector who will conduct your physical driving skill test. If passed, the inspector signs your file and forwards it to the printing section. You can check the printing and smart card dispatch status in Room 28 (DL branch office), collect the DL from the RTO inquiry counter after 10 days, or it will be sent to your registered address via Speed Post.

Driving License Renewal Process (Online & Offline): A driving license is valid for 20 years or until the age of 40, whichever is earlier. After age 40, DL must be renewed every 5 years. It can be renewed 1 year before or up to 1 year after expiry. Online: Apply on the Sarathi portal, select "DL Services", fill Form 9, upload old DL, and Form 1A medical certificate (if aged over 40 or commercial LMV), pay Rs. 400 (Rs. 200 renewal + Rs. 200 smart card fee) online. Offline: Submit Form 9 and Form 1A at the RTO Dehradun window along with the original DL, photos, address proof, and pay the fee in cash. If the DL has been expired for more than 1 year, a fresh driving skill test at the RTO track is mandatory.

Duplicate Driving License Application Process: If your driving license is lost, stolen, or completely damaged, you must apply for a duplicate DL. Online: On the Sarathi portal, select "Apply for Duplicate DL", enter DL details, upload a copy of the lost DL (if available), a copy of the Police FIR (mandatory for lost DL), and address proof. Pay the fee of Rs. 200 (or Rs. 400 with smart card fee) online. Offline: Go to RTO Dehradun, submit Form LLD (lost license declaration), copy of FIR, affidavit stating the DL is not impounded, damaged original DL (if damaged), and pay the fee at the counter.

International Driving Permit (IDP) Application Rules: To drive vehicles in a foreign country, you must apply for an International Driving Permit. An IDP is valid for 1 year from the date of issue or until the expiry of your Indian driving license, whichever is earlier. Online: On the Sarathi portal, select "Apply for International Driving Permit (IDP)", fill Form 4A, upload copies of your valid passport, visa, flight ticket, and valid Indian DL. Upload a medical certificate Form 1A signed by a doctor. Pay the fee of Rs. 1,000 online. Offline: Submit the physical Form 4A, Form 1A, photos, and passport/visa/DL photocopies to RTO Dehradun and pay the Rs. 1,000 fee. You must visit the RTO for document verification.

Addition of Class of Vehicle (COV) to DL: If you hold a two-wheeler license and want to add LMV (cars), you must apply for a Learner's License for LMV first. Go to Sarathi, apply for LL for LMV, pay Rs. 150 LL fee. After 30 days of holding the LMV LL, apply for DL Addition of Class, pay Rs. 250 fee, book the driving test slot for LMV, and pass the test at the RTO track.

Driving License Address Change or Correction Process (Online & Offline): To change your address or correct spelling mistakes in your DL, visit the DL Branch windows located in Rooms 25 and 28 at RTO Dehradun. Online: On the Sarathi portal, select 'Apply for Change of Address' or 'DL Services (Replace/Correction)', fill out the details, upload address proof, and pay the fee of Rs. 200. Offline: Submit a physical file containing your original DL, physical copy of address proof (e.g. Aadhaar Card), and payment receipt to the desk in Room 25 or 28.

Driving License Backlog Entry (Digital Conversion): If you hold an old manual paper booklet driving license and want to perform any transaction (like renewal or duplicate DL) online, you must first do a backlog entry to digitize your license records in the Sarathi database. Visit the DL Data Entry section in Room 28 at RTO Dehradun. Submit your physical original booklet license, local address proof, and a written application. The data entry operator will verify the physical records in the RTO register and approve the digital record entry.
