Temporary Vehicle Registration Rules: Temporary vehicle registration is issued by the dealer or RTO for a maximum validity of 1 month. It is meant to allow the vehicle to be driven to the registering authority. It cannot be renewed except under special circumstances approved by the RTO. The fee for temporary registration is Rs. 100.

New Vehicle Registration Process (Online vs. Offline): Online registration is the most common path for new private vehicles in Dehradun. The authorized vehicle dealer uploads the customer's Aadhaar, address proof, sale invoice, Form 21, and Form 22 to the Vahan portal. The customer pays the road tax and registration fees directly to the dealer (who submits them to the government). The dealer books the HSRP plate online. Once the HSRP is fitted, the registration is approved online, and the RC smart card is posted to the owner's home. Offline: If registering a custom vehicle or doing self-registration, the owner must take the new vehicle to RTO Dehradun. Purchase Form 20, fill it, and attach Form 21, Form 22, valid insurance, address proof, PAN card, and photos. Submit the file at Counter 3 (New vehicle registration counter). Pay the road tax (typically 8% to 12% for cars, 6% to 10% for bikes) and registration fee (Rs. 300 for two-wheelers, Rs. 600 for cars) at the cash counter. Take the vehicle to the RTO inspector at the inspection bay. The inspector will verify the chassis/engine pencil print. Once approved, the HSRP plate must be fitted at a dealer, and the smart card RC will be dispatched.

RC Transfer (Transfer of Vehicle Ownership) Online Process: Go to the Vahan Parivahan portal (vahan.parivahan.gov.in) and enter the vehicle registration number. Click on "Transfer of Ownership". The seller enters their details, uploads Form 29 (notice of transfer) and Form 30 (application for transfer) signed by both parties, along with valid insurance, PUC, and buyer's address proof. Pay the fee online (Rs. 150 for two-wheelers, Rs. 300 for LMV/cars). The buyer must accept the application online using their Aadhaar e-KYC. Once accepted, print the online payment receipt and the generated Form 29 and Form 30. Both buyer and seller must sign these printed copies and submit them physically to RTO Dehradun within 14 days, along with the original Registration Certificate (RC). The RTO will verify the signatures and approve the ownership transfer.

RC Transfer (Transfer of Vehicle Ownership) Offline Process: Gather all physical documents in a file: original RC, valid insurance photocopy, valid PUC certificate photocopy, self-attested ID and address proof of both buyer and seller, PAN card copies (or Form 60), 3 passport-size photos of the buyer, pencil print of the chassis number on paper, and financier NOC Form 35 (if the vehicle was under a loan/hypothecation). Visit the RTO Dehradun office. Purchase physical copies of Form 29 and Form 30, fill them out, and get signatures from both buyer and seller. Submit the complete physical file at the RTO document submission counter in Room 4. The clerk will verify the file and enter details into the Vahan system. Go to the cash counter and pay the transfer fee (Rs. 150 for bikes, Rs. 300 for cars) in cash. Collect the receipt. The transfer will be processed in the RTO database, and the new RC will be sent to the buyer's address.

Duplicate Registration Certificate (RC) Application Process (Online & Offline): Online: On the Vahan portal, select "Apply for Duplicate RC", enter vehicle details, select Form 26, upload a scanned copy of the Police FIR (mandatory for lost RC), an affidavit stating the loss, and valid insurance/PUC. Pay the fee (Rs. 150 for two-wheelers, Rs. 300 for cars) online. Submit the physical printout of the application receipt, FIR copy, and affidavit to RTO Dehradun for approval. Offline: Visit the Registration Branch in Room 4 at RTO Dehradun, purchase and fill Form 26, attach a copy of the lost RC FIR, an affidavit confirming the loss, valid insurance, PUC, address proof, and engine/chassis pencil print. Submit the file at the Room 4 counters, pay the fee in cash, and collect the receipt.

Hypothecation (Loan) Addition and Termination (Form 34 and 35): If you purchase a vehicle on a bank loan, hypothecation must be added to the RC. If the loan is fully repaid, hypothecation must be terminated. Apply online on the Vahan portal or offline at Room 4 (Registration Branch) at RTO Dehradun. For termination, submit Form 35 (two copies signed by the bank manager) along with the original bank No Objection Certificate (NOC), original RC, valid insurance, and PUC. Pay the fee (Rs. 100 for two-wheelers, Rs. 200 for cars) at the counter in Room 4. The RTO will issue a fresh clean RC without the bank's name.

Other-State Vehicle Re-registration (UK-07) Process: If you bring a vehicle registered in another state (e.g., Delhi DL, Haryana HR, Uttar Pradesh UP) to Dehradun, and plan to use it for more than 12 months, you must re-register it to get a UK-07 registration number. First, obtain a No Objection Certificate (NOC Form 28) from the original registering RTO in the home state. Apply on the Vahan portal or visit Room 4 (Registration Branch) at RTO Dehradun for private vehicles, submit Form 27 (application for assignment of new registration mark) along with the original NOC (Form 28), original RC, valid insurance, PUC, local Uttarakhand address proof, and chassis pencil print. Pay the recalculated road tax in Uttarakhand (calculated on the depreciated value of the vehicle) and the registration fee. Present the vehicle for physical inspection at the RTO Dehradun inspection bay. Once approved, the new UK-07 registration number is allocated, and the owner must fit new HSRP plates.

Registration Certificate (RC) Renewal Process (Online & Offline): The permanent registration of a private non-transport vehicle is valid for 15 years from the date of issue. After 15 years, it must be renewed every 5 years by undergoing a fitness inspection at the RTO. Online: Apply on the Vahan portal, fill Form 25, pay the fee online, and book the inspection appointment. Offline: Visit Room 4 at RTO Dehradun to submit the physical Form 25 file along with the original RC, valid insurance copy, valid PUC certificate copy, up-to-date road tax receipt, and a pencil print of the chassis number on paper. For both online and offline routes, you must physically present the vehicle at the RTO Dehradun inspection bay for inspector verification. The renewal fee is Rs. 300 for two-wheelers and Rs. 600 for cars. Private vehicle RCs can be renewed up to 1 year prior to expiry.
